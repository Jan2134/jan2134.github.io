My name is Jan. I grew up in Germany knowing I wanted to work on problems that mattered. I just hadn't figured out where, or how.

At 18, that became concrete. I chose Barcelona specifically for ESADE, one of Europe's top business schools, and for the city itself: international and entrepreneurial. I wanted an environment that would demand more of me. It delivered.

Two things grabbed me, and I've been following both ever since.

The first is technology. I took every programming elective ESADE offered, attended an AI summer school at Oxford, completed a data analytics programme with CBS Copenhagen, and stayed on for a Master's in Business Analytics. Python, SQL, R, and AWS became part of my weekly toolkit, not credentials on a page, but tools I actually use. Most evenings I'm building something: prototypes, apps, websites. I can't help it.

The second is what you do with it. I spent my university years doing pro-bono consulting through student organisations, leading teams of 15+, co-designing a financial literacy programme with an ESADE professor, and interning at Porsche back in Germany. But the education that stayed with me happened further afield. I consulted businesses in South Africa's townships outside Cape Town. I worked with local companies on the ground in Bolivia, did an exchange in Vancouver, and joined study tours in Seoul. Germany, Spain, the UK, Canada, South Korea, South Africa, Bolivia: seven countries across five continents before 25, each one teaching me something a classroom couldn't: that the right answer is almost never purely technical or purely strategic.

AuraEat

[ios_app] [app_store_soon]

A circadian nutrition concept built for Esade's Ideaup Start-Up Challenge, syncing your eating window to your biological clock via Apple HealthKit. Currently prepping for App Store release.

Reads sleep, HRV, and resting heart rate to compute your Aura Score, a daily readiness metric that determines the optimal eating window. Built with friends as a challenge project, no company behind it yet, but the plan is to get it live on the App Store.
